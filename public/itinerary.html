Day-by-Day Journey: Fort Worth, TX to Belgrade, MT (June 9 - June 19)

This itinerary is crafted with safety, comfort, and the well-being of myself and my furry companions in mind. Each day is planned to be manageable, allowing for ample rest and frequent stops. Click on the location names to open them in your default map application!

Day 1: Monday, June 9 - Fort Worth, TX to McAlester, OK

Driving Route: Depart Fort Worth, TX, and head northeast towards McAlester, OK. The most direct route is generally via US-377 N to US-69 N.

Distance: Approximately 196-205 miles. Estimated Driving Time: 3 hours 16 minutes to 3 hours 19 minutes.

Overnight Stay (Primary Recommendation):

  • Murphy's Meadow Camp Grounds (McAlester Army Ammunition Plant)
    • Amenities: Offers full hookups (water, sewer, electric), restrooms, showers, and a coin-operated laundry facility.
    • Pet Policy: Pets are welcome, but some restrictions apply; it is advisable to call for more details. Reviewers note staff are friendly and accommodating.
    • Cost: From $14 per night (military discount applies).

Backup Overnight Options:

  • Walmart Supercenter, McAlester, OK
  • Cracker Barrel, Shawnee, OK (approx. 1 hour north of McAlester)

Pet Stops/Breaks:

  • Love's Travel Stop Exit 137, Oklahoma City, OK
  • Love's Travel Stop, Newcastle, OK

Day 2: Tuesday, June 10 - Fort Worth, TX to Ozarks Area (Linn Creek, MO)

Driving Route: From Fort Worth, TX, head northeast towards Linn Creek, MO. The route typically involves US-377 N to US-69 N, then I-44 East and then US-54 East.

Distance: Approximately 300-350 miles. Estimated Driving Time: 5-6 hours.

Overnight Stay (Confirmed Reservation):

  • Lake of the Ozarks / Linn Creek KOA Holiday
    • Amenities: Offers RV sites with full hookups (electricity, water, sewer), on-site laundromat, and Wi-Fi.
    • Pet Policy: Pet-friendly with a "Kamp K9" area. Pets must be on a leash at all times, and Pit Bulls are not permitted. Proof of vaccinations may be requested.

Backup Overnight Options:

  • Lazy River RV Resort, Eldon, MO
  • Walmart Supercenter, Eldon, MO

Pet Stops/Breaks:

  • Plan for frequent stops at rest areas along I-44 and US-54. Many interstate rest areas provide designated pet relief areas.

Day 3-5: Wednesday, June 11 - Friday, June 13 - Extended Stay in Ozarks (Linn Creek, MO)

Base Camp: Continue the stay at Lake of the Ozarks / Linn Creek KOA Holiday, utilizing your confirmed reservation (Space 98, Confirmation #3540614). This provides a stable and comfortable environment with essential amenities (electricity, showers, laundry, Wi-Fi) crucial for the traveler's cancer recovery and for the dogs' comfort. Having a consistent base minimizes daily setup and breakdown stress.

Day Trip: Cemetery Visit (June 12):

  • Destination: Tuscumbia Cemetery, Tuscumbia, MO
    • Route from Linn Creek: Take US-54 East towards Eldon, then connect to Highway 52 South directly to Tuscumbia. The cemetery is easily located on the highway.

Local Exploration (Optional, Low-Stress):

  • Ha Ha Tonka State Park (Camdenton, MO)
  • Lake of the Ozarks State Park (Kaiser, MO)

Day 6: Saturday, June 14 - Ozarks Area (Linn Creek, MO) to Salina, KS

Driving Route: Depart Linn Creek, MO, and head west towards Salina, KS. The route will generally involve taking US-54 West, connecting to I-44 West, and then transitioning to I-70 West.

Distance: Approximately 260-280 miles. Estimated Driving Time: 4.5-5 hours.

Overnight Stay (Primary Recommendation):

  • COE Kanopolis Reservoir Riverside Campground, Marquette, KS
    • Amenities: This Army Corps of Engineers park offers campsites, many with electric and water hookups, as well as restrooms and showers. It is situated on flat terrain with grass and scattered trees.
    • Pet Policy: Generally pet-friendly, but leash rules apply.
    • Cost: Fees vary by hookup type; for example, $20/night for 30 amp/water, $18/night for 30 amp, or $14/night for no hookups.

Backup Overnight Options:

  • Walmart Supercenter, Salina, KS
  • Cracker Barrel, Salina, KS

Pet Stops/Breaks:

  • Numerous rest areas are available along I-70 in Kansas. Many of these rest areas are dog-friendly and provide designated pet areas for exercise and potty breaks.

Day 7: Sunday, June 15 - Salina, KS to North Platte, NE

Driving Route: From Salina, KS, head northwest towards North Platte, NE. The route involves I-70 West, then US-183 North, connecting to I-80 West in Nebraska.

Distance: Approximately 297-306 miles. Estimated Driving Time: 4 hours 38 minutes to 4 hours 45 minutes.

Overnight Stay (Primary Recommendation):

  • Cabela's RV Park & Campground, Sidney, NE (Note: Sidney is approximately 1 hour west of North Platte, but offers a dedicated, reliable campground option).
    • Amenities: This is a full-service campground with electric, water, and sewer hookups, restrooms, and showers. It also features a dump station and a general store.
    • Pet Policy: Pets are allowed. Some Cabela's locations even offer dog kennels.
    • Cost: Full-service sites are $40 per day (peak season). Discounts are available for AAA, AARP, and Good Sam Club members (10%), and Cabela's/Bass Pro CLUB Members (20%).

Backup Overnight Options:

  • North Platte Walmart Supercenter, North Platte, NE
  • Ogallala I-80 KOA Journey, Ogallala, NE (Approximately 50 miles west of North Platte, closer than Sidney if preferred).

Pet Stops/Breaks:

  • Numerous rest areas are available along I-80 in Nebraska. Many of these, such as the Sidney Welcome Center, Platte River Rest Area, and Ogallala Rest Area, are designated pet areas.

Day 8: Monday, June 16 - North Platte, NE to Laramie, WY

Driving Route: From North Platte, NE, continue west on I-80 towards Laramie, WY.

Distance: Approximately 269 miles. Estimated Driving Time: 4 hours 2 minutes.

Overnight Stay (Primary Recommendation):

  • F.E. Warren AFB FamCamp, Cheyenne, WY (Cheyenne is approximately 36 miles east of Laramie, providing a military option).
    • Amenities: Offers 40 full hook-up sites (30 and 50 amp service), laundry facilities, showers, restrooms, and Wi-Fi internet service.
    • Pet Policy: Pets are allowed with usual leash and clean-up rules.
    • Cost: Full Hook-Up sites are $35/night. Overflow/dry camping is $15/night. Costs double during Cheyenne Frontier Days (typically 3rd and 4th week of July).

Backup Overnight Options:

  • Laramie Walmart Supercenter, Laramie, WY
  • Curt Gowdy State Park, Cheyenne, WY (Approximately 18 miles from Laramie).

Pet Stops/Breaks:

  • Little America Travel Center, Little America, WY
  • Rest areas along I-80 in Wyoming are generally pet-friendly.

Day 9-10: Tuesday, June 17 - Wednesday, June 18 - Laramie, WY (Rest & Preparation)

Base Camp: Continue the stay at F.E. Warren AFB FamCamp, Cheyenne, WY. This location offers a secure and amenity-rich environment that is ideal for extended rest and preparation before the final leg of the journey. The availability of full hookups, laundry, and showers provides a comfortable "home base" for recovery and re-energizing.

Activities:

  • Prioritize Rest and Recovery: The primary focus for these two days should be on rest and physical recovery, especially given the cancer recovery status. Avoid strenuous activities.
  • Resupply: Utilize local grocery stores and pharmacies in Cheyenne or Laramie to restock food, water, medications, and pet supplies.
  • Laundry: Take advantage of the FamCamp's laundry facilities.
  • Van Maintenance: Conduct basic checks of the van (tire pressure, fluid levels) and address any minor issues that may have arisen during the drive. This is also an opportunity to clean the van's interior.
  • Optional Light Exploration (if energy permits): If the traveler feels up to it, light, low-stress exploration of Laramie or Cheyenne could be considered, such as visiting a local park for a gentle stroll with the dogs. However, this should be entirely optional and secondary to rest.
  • Pet Care: These days provide an excellent opportunity for the dogs to have extended playtime and walks outside the confines of the van. The FamCamp's pet-friendly environment allows for regular exercise.

Day 11: Thursday, June 19 - Laramie, WY to Belgrade, MT

Driving Route: Depart Laramie, WY, and head north towards Belgrade, MT. The route generally involves I-80 West briefly, then I-25 North, and finally I-90 West. This is the longest driving day of the itinerary.

Distance: Approximately 295 miles. Estimated Driving Time: 5 hours 52 minutes. An early start is highly recommended to complete this leg comfortably.

Overnight Stay (Primary Recommendation):

  • Three Forks KOA Journey, Three Forks, MT (Approximately 22 miles from Belgrade).
    • Amenities: Offers full hookups (electricity, water, sewer), laundry facilities, and showers. KOA campgrounds are known for providing clean, level sites.
    • Pet Policy: KOA campgrounds are generally pet-friendly, offering amenities like Kamp K9 areas. Pets typically need to be on a leash.

Backup Overnight Options:

  • Bozeman Walmart Supercenter, Bozeman, MT (Approximately 10 miles from Belgrade).
  • Cabela's, Billings, MT (Approximately 90 miles east of Bozeman, a longer drive back if the traveler needs to stop earlier, but a known option).

Pet Stops/Breaks:

  • Rest areas along I-90 in Montana, such as the Bozeman (N. 19th Interchange) Rest Area, are available for pet breaks.
